<p><p>Electroceuticals medicine encompasses two primary market types: implantable electroceuticals devices and non-invasive electroceuticals devices. Implantable electroceuticals devices, such as pacemakers and neurostimulators, are surgically placed within the body to regulate physiological functions and treat conditions like chronic pain and neurological disorders. In contrast, non-invasive electroceuticals devices, including transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) units and neuromodulation devices, provide therapeutic effects through external application, enhancing patient comfort while addressing various health issues without surgical intervention.</p></p>
