<p><p>The automotive all-wheel drive (AWD) market features two main types: automatic and manual AWD systems. Automatic AWD systems use sensors and electronics to detect wheel slip, adjusting power distribution between front and rear wheels for optimal traction without driver intervention. In contrast, manual AWD systems require drivers to engage or disengage the all-wheel drive mode, providing more control in specific conditions but demanding active management. Both types cater to various consumer preferences for performance, off-road capability, and driving experience.</p></p>
