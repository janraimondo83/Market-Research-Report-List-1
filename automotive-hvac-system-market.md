<p><p>The Automotive HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) system regulates cabin temperature and air quality. **Standalone HVAC** systems operate independently, with dedicated controls and components, offering flexibility and customization for specific vehicle models. **Dependent HVAC** systems integrate with the vehicle's existing systems, relying on shared controls and components, often reducing costs and complexity. Standalone systems are common in premium or specialized vehicles, while dependent systems are widely used in mass-market cars for efficiency and affordability. Both types aim to enhance passenger comfort and climate control.</p></p>
