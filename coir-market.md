<p><ul><li>Brown Fiber</li><li>White Fiber</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The coir market primarily consists of two types: brown fiber and white fiber. Brown fiber is derived from mature coconuts, known for its strength and durability, making it ideal for industrial applications like ropes, mats, and brushes. White fiber, extracted from immature coconuts, is softer, finer, and used in premium products like mattresses, upholstery, and textiles. Both fibers cater to distinct markets, with brown fiber dominating heavy-duty uses and white fiber appealing to high-end, comfort-driven industries.</p></p>
