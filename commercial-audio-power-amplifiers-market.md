<p><p>Commercial audio power amplifiers are categorized into several types based on their operation and efficiency. Class A amplifiers provide high sound fidelity but are less efficient, generating more heat. Class B amplifiers improve efficiency but can introduce distortion during operation. Class AB combines Class A and B characteristics, offering a balance of sound quality and efficiency. Class D amplifiers use pulse-width modulation for higher efficiency and lower heat generation, making them suitable for compact applications. Other types may include hybrid or specialized designs.</p></p>
